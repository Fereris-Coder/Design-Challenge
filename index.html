Redesign Proposal

Haru: Finance with Purpose and Zero Friction

Transforming raw numbers into intelligent decisions through automation and emotional support for the Ecuadorian market.

The Challenge

Help users understand their money without the friction of manual categorization and with truly actionable insights.

Our Vision

An app that doesn't feel like a ledger, but rather like an empathetic and intuitive financial copilot.

Context & Reasoning

What do we understand about Haru's users?

We analyzed the friction points detected in the initial research and proposed clear design hypotheses.

User Pain Point
Hypothesis of Solution in Haru
"I struggle to understand where my money goes each month."
Contextual Visualization: Clear visual grouping and narrative summaries (e.g. "You are doing 10% better than last week").
"I don't have the time or desire to manually categorize expenses."
Auto-Categorization with Express Confirmation: The app does it for you and validates with simple 1-tap interactions.
"I don't feel the app gives me useful insights, just numbers."
Financial Health Indicator & AI Copilot: Empathetic comments explaining what to do (e.g. "If you save $15 on entertainment, you will reach your goal").
Lateral Thinking

Questioning the Financial Status Quo

Do users really need perfect pie charts and tedious manual bookkeeping?

💡
Our Lateral Hypothesis: The user does not want to be an accountant. They want peace of mind. Categorization is not the end goal, it is just the tool. By automating 95% of the mental load and providing direct answers about daily spending power, we eliminate app churn.

UX/UI Decisions

Emotional & Functional Aesthetics

We aim to project financial security through a modern, clean, and motivating interface.

UI Decisions

  • Trust Palette: Deep purples, emerald greens for growth, and subtle accents for warnings.
  • Micro-animations: Smooth feedback animations on verification buttons to build satisfaction.
  • Information Cards: Glassmorphism layouts to add visual hierarchy and modern depth.

UX Decisions

  • Human Language: Translating cold figures to everyday milestones ("You have enough to go out this weekend!").
  • Focus on Free Margin: Highlighting free money after fixed expenses and savings goals to reduce financial anxiety.
  • Asynchronous Batching: Allowing incorrect categorizations to be fixed in batches whenever the user wishes.
